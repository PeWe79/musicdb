MusicDB


MusicDB is a music manager, server and player that focus on music. It is more like a music presenter than a database frontend.
Download

Introduction

MusicDB is a music manager, server and player that focus is on music, not the software itself. It manages music on a server following the concept The Filesystem is Always Right; the Database is Just for Augmentation. So, the tidiness of you music is not just inside MusicDB but on all devices like your portable music-player or your car.

You can stream your music by Music Playing Daemon (MPD). This stream and can be received by all kind of players like iTunes, VLC or by Icecast to encrypt the stream and send it out into the internet. So your music is in the cloud but your files stay at home.

The stream gets controlled by a WebUI that hides the uglyness of software behind a nice, music-oriented and music-presenting frontend. Your music gets presented and is not just a database entry in a boring list of artists and albums.

It's beautiful

The WebUI presents the music, mostly represented by their album artwork. Instead of most other music manager and player, the management part of the music is totally hidden. This UI is made to enjoy listening to music, not to work on managing it. It adopts it colors to the color theme of the album artwork and allows making excessive use of Unicode.

There are four important panes in the WebUI:

  1. HUD (top): Showing the current playing song and allows to quick access flags.
  2. Artist List (left): A list of artists and their albums
  3. Album View (middle): Album of the current playing song, or selected from the list on the left.
  4. Queue View (right): The current queue of songs that will be played.

Head-up Display

The head-up display presents the current playing song. It also shows its tags and mood-flags. The mood-flags represent different moods the user/listener as associates with this song. Furthermore there are buttons to mark this song as favorite or hated. On the right side, the current time and the time when the queue runs empty is shown. So, you can see when you can sleep, or if it is worth adding some more songs to the queue.


Artist List

The artist list shows all albums that are in the genres that are selected in the genre filter box above. The albums are grouped by their artists and sorted by release date. By clicking on the album, it gets presented in the Album View.

Albums can have one or more main genre like "Metal", "Classic", …. If you want to listen to Metal, you simply select the Metal genre and disable all other. Then only Metal albums are listed. When you select multiple genre, all albums that belong to at least one of the activated genres are shown.

Fuzzy Search

Beside selecting an album from Artist List and then choose a song, you can use a search to find and add a certain song directly. The search finds all songs, albums and artists that matches the enters string to a certain probability. While typing the name, a preview of the results with the five most probable hits gets shown in real time.


Lyrics Management

Beside the audio and artwork, lyrics are the third part of a music collection. MusicDB comes with a simple lyrics management allowing you to annotate lyrics to songs.

Album View

The Album View show the current playing album or a selected album from the Artist List. In this view, all songs of an album get presented. Beside the song name, their mood flags are displayed. The diamantes on the screenshot show that these songs are favorite songs. Furthermore on the very right of each song entry, a "likes/dislikes-bar" is shown. These information help choosing songs from albums you haven't listen to for a long time.

Right click on the album title allows you setting the genre tags of an album and the colors that will be used for the WebUI when showing this album. Right click on a song opens a control box similar to the HUD. There you can set the genre of a song, change flags or direct listen to the song in your browser.

Artwork management

Album artwork is an important part of music. So, the management does not only concentrates on the music but also on the artwork.

For each album are three colors defined that will be used for the whole WebUI. So, the whole UIs style fits to the design of the album artwork.


Queue

MusicDB does not use playlists, it uses a queue. Songs can be added at the end of the queue, or on top right after the current playing song. The queue view also allows to remove songs from the queue or change their position by moving them via drag 'n' drop. When the queue runs empty, a pseudo random song gets added. So, the music never stops playing and the is no repeating playlist annoying you.

Subjective Randomizer

While real randomness allows to play the same song of thousands several times in succession, the configurable random song selector from MusicDB does not add songs to the queue that are too short, flagged as hated or when another song of the same album or artist was recently played.

Further Features

Manage - Present - Listen

Most of the music management takes place on command line using MusicDBs command line modules.

Managing the queue of the songs that shall be streamed will be done using MusicDBs Web UI that presents the music collection.

Listening to the stream can be done by any player that supports streams, like VLC with its powerful signal processing capability.

Your Music Everywhere

Due to the fact that this is a server/client application, you can listen to your music wherever there is an internet connection. The music is stored on the server MusicDB runs on. The queue of the music that gets streamed can be managed from anywhere via browser. And each device that can play audio streams can be used to listen to that stream.

Global state

The state of MusicDB is stored in the server. So, all clients connected to the server see the same information. This is not a multi-user application. All clients see the same queue, and the same album gets shown in the album view. Updating a flag or setting a tag gets immediately visible to all clients.

Powerful export

MusicDB provides a powerful export functionality that allows you to listen to your music in your car that only supports mp3 encoded files, or to pre-scale the artworks so that your portable audio player scrolls smoother through your albums. You can also add or remove the artwork from files, or change the file name encoding in case you have to export it to an ancient filesystem that does not support Unicode.

Neural Network based auto tagging

MusicDB comes with the capability to use a Convolutional Deep Neural Network to classify (tag) your music. The AI can "listen" to a song and sets one or more genre tags for the song. This tag can be confirmed or rejected by the user when it gets displayed in the WebUI. On the screenshots above, you will notice those AI set tags by their "probability-bar"

Because each music collection is different, there comes no pre-trained model. In case you do not want to spend the effort to train a model, the AI functionality can be ignored. The AI is disabled by default. This is a Crazy-Nerd Feature

Well Documented

This does not sound relevant, but it is a very important featured. The whole server code is well documented including all interfaces. That makes it very easy to develop own frontends that fits perfect to your music listening behavior. And if there is a feature missing it is easy to add.
